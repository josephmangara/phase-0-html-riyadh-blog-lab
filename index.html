Once a mudbrick waystation along trading routes, Riyadh is now one of the wealthiest cities in the world. The country's political, financial and administration capital is also a city with a fascinating subtext.

History

Riyadh (/ree.ad/; Arabic:الرياض,"The Gardens") is the capital and largest city of Saudi Arabia. It is also the capital of Riyadh province, and belongs to the historical regions of Najd and Al-Yamama. It is situated in the center of the Arabian Peninsula on a large plateau, and is home to 5,254,899 people, and the urban center of region of close to 7 million people.

The city is divided into 15 municipal districts managed by Riyadh Municipality headed by the mayor of Riyadh, and the Riyadh development authority, chaired by the governor, Khalid bin Bandar Al Saud. The current mayor of Riyadh is Abdul Rahman Al Mogbel, appointed in 2012. Riyadh has been designated as a Beta World City.
